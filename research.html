Unsupervised Representation Learning for 3D Morphological Analysis

Theory@EMBL Fellow · European Molecular Biology Laboratory, Heidelberg

Developing a modality-agnostic deep learning framework for quantitative morphological analysis of ultrastructure expansion microscopy (U-ExM) data — without manual segmentation or labeled training data. The approach uses variational autoencoders and self-supervised architectures to extract morphological features directly from 3D image volumes, enabling large-scale phenotyping of centrin structural diversity across free-living unicellular eukaryotes distributed throughout the tree of life.
