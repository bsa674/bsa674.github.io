About me

Computational biologist working at the frontier of machine learning and life sciences.

bsamanta674@gmail.com · Heidelberg, DE

I am a Theory@EMBL Fellow and Scientific Trainee at the European Molecular Biology Laboratory (EMBL) in Heidelberg, where I develop unsupervised representation learning frameworks for 3D morphological analysis of ultrastructure expansion microscopy (U-ExM) data.

My work eliminates the need for manual segmentation or labeled training sets — making quantitative cell biology scalable across imaging modalities. I am currently investigating centrin structural diversity across free-living unicellular eukaryotes spanning the tree of life.

Previously, I was a Student Research Assistant at the Max Planck Institute for Multidisciplinary Sciences in Göttingen, building AlphaFold-based structural prediction pipelines integrated with transcriptomic analysis in Dr. Peter Lenart's lab. I completed an ERASMUS+ internship at IIMCB Warsaw, where I built an RNA 3D structure prediction meta-server that reduced processing time by 40% through parallel computing optimization.

My first publication — an explainable ML pipeline for antimicrobial resistance prediction — appeared in Bioinformatics Advances (Oxford Academic, 2025).
